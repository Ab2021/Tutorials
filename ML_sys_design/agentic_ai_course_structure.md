# Agentic AI Systems: Design, Engineering, and Real-World Applications (Detailed 30-Day Plan)

## Course Overview

This 30-day intensive course provides a comprehensive, engineering-focused guide to designing, building, and deploying agentic AI systems. We will journey from the foundational concepts of AI agents to the cutting-edge of Large Language Models (LLMs), covering system architecture, advanced engineering techniques, real-world applications, and the challenges of building robust, scalable, and ethical AI. The course culminates in a rigorous preparation for the agentic AI system design and engineering interview, equipping you with the deep technical knowledge required to excel.

---

### **Part 1: Foundations of Intelligent Agents (7 Days)**

*   **Day 1: The Dawn of Agentic AI**
    *   **Topic:** Introduction to Agentic AI and its significance.
    *   **Sub-topics:**
        *   What is an AI Agent? The shift from predictive models to autonomous systems.
        *   A Brief History of AI Agents: From GOFAI to Modern LLM-powered agents.
        *   Why Now? The confluence of LLMs, compute, and data.
        *   Real-world examples of agentic systems changing industries.
    *   **Activity:** Deconstruct a simple agentic system (e.g., a smart thermostat) into its core components.

*   **Day 2: The Agent's World**
    *   **Topic:** Core Concepts: Agents, Environments, Actions, Percepts (PEAS).
    *   **Sub-topics:**
        *   **Percepts:** How agents perceive their environment (text, images, APIs).
        *   **Environments:** Properties of environments (static vs. dynamic, discrete vs. continuous, single-agent vs. multi-agent).
        *   **Actions:** The agent's capabilities and how it affects the environment.
        *   **Sensors and Actuators:** The agent's interface with its world.
    *   **Activity:** Define the PEAS framework for various agent types (e.g., a web scraping bot, a customer service agent).

*   **Day 3: Inside the Agent's Mind**
    *   **Topic:** Agent Architectures.
    *   **Sub-topics:**
        *   Simple Reflex Agents.
        *   Model-based Reflex Agents.
        *   Goal-based Agents.
        *   Utility-based Agents.
        *   Learning Agents.
    *   **Activity:** Design a simple agent architecture for a given problem (e.g., a spam filter).

*   **Day 4: The Rise of LLMs**
    *   **Topic:** Introduction to Large Language Models (LLMs) as the core of modern agents.
    *   **Sub-topics:**
        *   What are LLMs and why are they a game-changer for agentic AI?
        *   High-level overview of the Transformer architecture.
        *   The concept of "foundation models".
        *   Prompt Engineering as the new way to program.
    *   **Activity:** Hands-on lab with a popular LLM API (e.g., OpenAI, Gemini) to solve simple tasks.

*   **Day 5: Week 1 in Review**
    *   **Topic:** Review, Q&A, and Project Ideation.
    *   **Sub-topics:**
        *   Recap of week 1 concepts.
        *   Open Q&A session.
        *   **Course Project:** Introduction to the course-long project. Students will start brainstorming and scoping their own agentic AI system.
*   **Day 6: The Engine of Intelligence**
    *   **Topic:** Deep Dive into LLMs.
    *   **Sub-topics:**
        *   The Transformer Architecture in detail: Self-Attention, Positional Encodings.
        *   Fine-tuning vs. In-context learning.
        *   When to fine-tune: Domain adaptation, style transfer.
        *   Techniques for efficient fine-tuning (LoRA, QLoRA).
    *   **Activity:** Fine-tune a small LLM on a specific dataset.

*   **Day 7: Memory and Learning**
    *   **Topic:** Memory and Learning in Agents.
    *   **Sub-topics:**
        *   The need for long-term memory.
        *   Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) for external knowledge.
        *   Vector Databases (e.g., Pinecone, Chroma) and their role.
        *   Building a simple RAG pipeline.
    *   **Activity:** Implement a RAG system to answer questions from a knowledge base.

### **Part 2: Engineering Agentic Systems (10 Days)**

*   **Day 8: The Art of Reasoning**
    *   **Topic:** Planning and Reasoning in Agents.
    *   **Sub-topics:**
        *   Chain of Thought (CoT) prompting.
        *   Tree of Thought (ToT) for exploring multiple reasoning paths.
        *   Graph of Thought (GoT) for more complex planning.
        *   Self-Correction and Self-Critique.
    *   **Activity:** Compare the performance of CoT and ToT on a complex reasoning task.

*   **Day 9: Agents with Tools**
    *   **Topic:** Tool Use and Function Calling.
    *   **Sub-topics:**
        *   Why agents need tools (e.g., calculators, search engines, APIs).
        *   The ReAct (Reason + Act) framework.
        *   How modern LLMs support function calling.
        *   Designing and implementing custom tools for your agent.
    *   **Activity:** Build an agent that can use a calculator and a weather API.

*   **Day 10: Many Minds, One Goal**
    *   **Topic:** Multi-Agent Systems.
    *   **Sub-topics:**
        *   Competition vs. Collaboration among agents.
        *   Architectures for multi-agent systems (e.g., hierarchical, broadcast).
        *   Communication protocols between agents.
        *   Use cases: simulated environments, complex problem solving.
    *   **Activity:** Simulate a simple multi-agent system (e.g., two agents negotiating a price).
*   **Day 11: The Blueprint for Intelligence**
    *   **Topic:** System Architecture Patterns for Agentic AI.
    *   **Sub-topics:**
        *   The "LLM as the brain" pattern.
        *   Event-driven architectures for agents.
        *   Microservices vs. Monolith for agentic systems.
        *   Data flow and state management.
    *   **Activity:** Design the high-level architecture for your course project.

*   **Day 12: Building for a Billion**
    *   **Topic:** Scalability and Performance.
    *   **Sub-topics:**
        *   Caching strategies for LLM responses.
        *   Load balancing and horizontal scaling.
        *   Optimizing LLM inference (quantization, distillation).
        *   Cost management and estimation.
    *   **Activity:** Analyze the cost and performance trade-offs of different LLMs.

*   **Day 13: Watching the Watchers**
    *   **Topic:** Observability for AI Agents.
    *   **Sub-topics:**
        *   Logging agent's thoughts and actions.
        *   Tracing the flow of information through the system.
        *   Metrics for agent performance (e.g., task success rate, tool usage).
        *   Debugging strategies for non-deterministic systems.
    *   **Activity:** Instrument an agent with logging and tracing.

*   **Day 14: Fortifying the Mind**
    *   **Topic:** Security for AI Agents.
    *   **Sub-topics:**
        *   Prompt Injection: The new SQL injection.
        *   Data privacy and sandboxing.
        *   Guarding against malicious tool use.
        *   Red teaming and security testing for agents.
    *   **Activity:** Attempt a prompt injection attack on a vulnerable agent.

*   **Day 15: Week 2 & 3 in Review**
    *   **Topic:** Review and Project Milestone 1.
    *   **Sub-topics:**
        *   Recap of engineering concepts.
        *   Open Q&A.
        *   **Project Milestone 1:** Present your agent's architecture and implementation plan.
*   **Day 16: The Empathetic Machine**
    *   **Topic:** Customer Service Bots that Don't Suck.
    *   **Case Study:** Analyze a real-world customer service agent (e.g., Intercom, Zendesk).
    *   **Design Challenge:** Design an agent that can handle complex customer queries and escalate to a human when needed.

*   **Day 17: The AI Co-pilot**
    *   **Topic:** AI for Code Generation and Software Development.
    *   **Case Study:** Deep dive into GitHub Copilot.
    *   **Design Challenge:** Design an agent that can write unit tests for a given piece of code.

### **Part 3: Real-World Applications and Case Studies (8 Days)**

*   **Day 18: The Digital Scientist**
    *   **Topic:** Agentic AI in Scientific Discovery and Research.
    *   **Case Study:** How agents are being used for drug discovery or materials science.
    *   **Design Challenge:** Design an agent that can read scientific papers and summarize the key findings.

*   **Day 19: The Creative Spark**
    *   **Topic:** Creative Agents: Art, Music, and Storytelling.
    *   **Case Study:** Analyze a creative AI tool (e.g., Midjourney, Suno).
    *   **Design Challenge:** Design an agent that can generate a short story with a consistent plot and characters.

*   **Day 20: Into the Trenches**
    *   **Topic:** Deep Dive Case Study.
    *   **Case Study:** A comprehensive analysis of a state-of-the-art agentic system (e.g., Devin, a complex financial analysis agent).
    *   **Activity:** Reverse-engineer the system's architecture, data flow, and potential challenges.
*   **Day 21: The Grand Challenge**
    *   **Topic:** The Agentic AI System Design Interview.
    *   **Sub-topics:**
        *   Frameworks for answering system design questions (e.g., PEDALS, STAR).
        *   Clarifying requirements and scoping the problem.
        *   High-level design and component selection.
        *   Deep dive into specific components.
        *   Identifying bottlenecks and trade-offs.

*   **Day 22: Mock Interview 1**
    *   **Topic:** Design a Personalized Shopping Assistant.
    *   **Activity:** Live mock interview with a student. The class provides feedback and discusses alternative solutions.

*   **Day 23: Mock Interview 2**
    *   **Topic:** Design a Complex Data Analysis Agent.
    *   **Activity:** Live mock interview with another student, focusing on a more data-intensive and analytical problem.

*   **Day 24: Dodging the Bullets**
    *   **Topic:** Common Pitfalls and How to Avoid Them.
    *   **Sub-topics:**
        *   Hand-waving over the LLM's capabilities.
        *   Ignoring scalability and cost.
        *   Forgetting about security and ethics.
        *   Not considering the user experience.

*   **Day 25: The Next Frontier**
    *   **Topic:** Advanced System Design.
    *   **Sub-topics:**
        *   Designing for distributed agents.
        *   Real-time processing and low-latency agents.
        *   Human-in-the-loop and hybrid intelligence systems.

---

### **Part 4: The Interview Gauntlet (5 Days)**

*   **Day 26: The Unanswerable Questions**
    *   **Topic:** Unique Interview Questions and How to Approach Them.
    *   **Sub-topics:**
        *   "How would you build an agent to... (do something seemingly impossible)?"
        *   "What is the most interesting agentic system you can imagine?"
        *   "How do you think agents will evolve in the next 5 years?"
    *   **Activity:** Brainstorming and discussing creative and insightful answers.

*   **Day 27: The Moral Compass**
    *   **Topic:** Complex Issues: Ethics, Bias, and Alignment.
    *   **Sub-topics:**
        *   Ensuring fairness and mitigating bias in agentic systems.
        *   The "alignment problem" and its implications.
        *   Designing for transparency and explainability.
        *   Ethical considerations in autonomous systems.

*   **Day 28: Telling Your Story**
    *   **Topic:** The Behavioral Interview for AI Engineers.
    *   **Sub-topics:**
        *   Crafting compelling stories about your projects and experiences.
        *   Using the STAR method to structure your answers.
        *   Demonstrating passion, curiosity, and a growth mindset.
    *   **Activity:** Practice answering common behavioral questions.

*   **Day 29: The Grand Finale**
    *   **Topic:** Project Showcase and Final Presentations.
    *   **Activity:** Students present their course projects to the class. Peer feedback and instructor evaluation.

*   **Day 30: The Road Ahead**
    *   **Topic:** Course Wrap-up, Career Advice, and Next Steps.
    *   **Sub-topics:**
        *   Review of key takeaways from the course.
        *   How to build a compelling portfolio.
        *   Networking and finding opportunities in the agentic AI space.
        *   Continuing education and staying up-to-date.
        *   Final Q&A.
